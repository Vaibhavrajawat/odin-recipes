Butter Paneer Recipe

Description

Butter Paneer, also known as Paneer Makhani, is a rich and creamy North Indian dish that combines soft paneer cubes with a luscious tomato-based sauce.
It's a favorite in Indian cuisine, perfect for a comforting meal with naan or rice. Here's a detailed guide to making this delicious dish at home.


Ingredients

For the Paneer:

  • 250 grams of paneer, cut into cubes
  • 2 tablespoons of butter
  • 1 tablespoon of oil
  • 1/2 teaspoon of turmeric powder
  • Salt to taste
For the Gravy:

  • 2 tablespoons of butter
  • 1 tablespoon of oil
  • 1 large onion
  • finely chopped 3-4 tomatoes
  • pureed 1 tablespoon of ginger-garlic paste
  • 2-3 green chilies
  • slit (optional)
  • 1 teaspoon of cumin seeds
  • 1 bay leaf 2-3 cloves
  • 1/2 teaspoon of turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon of red chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon of garam masala
  • 1/2 teaspoon of coriander powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon of kasuri methi (dried fenugreek leaves)
  • 1/2 cup of fresh cream
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh cilantro for garnish
  • 1 teaspoon of sugar (optional)


Detailed Steps

Preparing the Paneer:

Heat 2 tablespoons of butter and 1 tablespoon of oil in a pan. Add paneer cubes, turmeric powder, and salt. Sauté until the paneer is lightly golden. Set aside.

Making the Gravy:

In the same pan, add 2 tablespoons of butter and 1 tablespoon of oil. Add cumin seeds, bay leaf, and cloves. Sauté for a minute until aromatic. Add finely chopped onions and sauté until golden brown. Stir in the ginger-garlic paste and green chilies. Cook for 2-3 minutes.

Add tomato puree and cook until the oil separates from the mixture. Mix in the turmeric powder, red chili powder, garam masala, and coriander powder. Cook for another 2-3 minutes.

Combining Paneer and Gravy:

Add the sautéed paneer cubes to the gravy. Stir gently to coat the paneer with the sauce. Crush kasuri methi between your palms and add it to the gravy. Mix well. Pour in the fresh cream and stir until the sauce is smooth and creamy. Season with salt and add sugar if you prefer a slightly sweeter taste. Final Touch: Garnish with fresh cilantro and an extra drizzle of cream.

Serving:

Serve hot with naan, roti, or steamed rice. Enjoy your homemade Butter Paneer! This dish is best enjoyed fresh, with the creamy gravy soaking into the soft paneer, offering a delightful taste experience. This recipe brings the flavors of a classic Indian restaurant dish into your kitchen. With its rich and creamy texture, Butter Paneer is sure to be a hit with anyone who loves Indian cuisine.
