Butter Paneer Recipe

Description

Chole Bhature is a beloved dish in Indian cuisine, known for its combination of soft, fluffy Bhature and tangy, spicy Chole. The Chole, made from chickpeas, is simmered in a rich, aromatic gravy with tomatoes and a blend of spices.
The Bhature is a deep-fried bread, crispy on the outside and soft on the inside, perfect for scooping up the delicious Chole. This dish is often served with pickles, yogurt, and salad, making it a complete meal.


Ingredients

For Chole (Chickpeas Curry):

  • 1 cup dried chickpeas (soaked overnight)
  • 2 tablespoons oil
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, pureed
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 2-3 green chilies, slit (optional)
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 2 teaspoons chole masala powder
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon cumin powder
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1 teaspoon amchur (dry mango powder)
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh cilantro for garnish
For Bhature:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 tablespoons semolina (optional)
  • 1/2 cup yogurt
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • Water as needed
  • Oil for deep frying


Detailed Steps

Preparing Chole:
    Cook the Chickpeas:
    Drain and rinse the soaked chickpeas. Boil them with enough water until soft (you can also use a pressure cooker). Set aside.

    Making the Gravy:
    Heat oil in a pan. Add cumin seeds and bay leaf.
    Add chopped onions and saut√© until golden brown.
    Stir in ginger-garlic paste and green chilies. Cook for a few minutes.
    Add tomato puree and cook until the oil separates from the mixture.
    Add chole masala, red chili powder, turmeric powder, cumin powder, and coriander powder. Cook for another 2-3 minutes.

    Combine and Simmer:
    Add the cooked chickpeas to the pan. Mix well.
    Add water as needed to adjust the consistency of the gravy. Let it simmer for 15-20 minutes.
    Stir in garam masala and amchur powder. Adjust salt to taste.

    Garnish:
    Garnish with fresh cilantro.
Preparing Bhature:
    Make the Dough: In a bowl, mix all-purpose flour, semolina, baking powder, baking soda, sugar, and salt.
    Add yogurt and mix. Gradually add water to knead a soft dough. Cover and let it rest for 1-2 hours.
    Shape and Fry: Divide the dough into small balls. Roll each ball into a flat disc (about 1/4 inch thick).
    Heat oil in a deep frying pan. Fry the Bhature until golden brown and puffed. Drain on paper towels.
Serving:
Serve the hot Bhature with the spicy Chole. Add slices of onion, lemon wedges, and a sprinkle of fresh cilantro on the Chole. You can also serve with pickles and yogurt on the side for an extra flavor burst.
